# Business Plan

## Company Overview
Company name: CyberBerlin Robotics GmbH
Registered seat: Berlin
Founder: Roger Binny
Legal form: GmbH

## Vision
CyberBerlin Robotics GmbH builds robotics systems that help industrial and logistics teams automate repetitive, precision-heavy workflows with safer, more adaptive software and machine intelligence. The long-term vision is to become a Berlin-based robotics technology company that bridges physical automation, perception, and deployment tooling for European SMEs.

## Problem
Many manufacturing, warehouse, and applied robotics environments still rely on fragmented automation stacks, manual interventions, and expensive custom integration work. Smaller companies often cannot afford large bespoke robotics programmes, yet they still face labor shortages, quality variation, and scaling bottlenecks.

## Solution
CyberBerlin Robotics GmbH will develop modular robotics software and deployment services that enable customers to:
- automate repetitive industrial tasks faster
- integrate robotics hardware with modern cloud and data workflows
- reduce setup time through reusable software components
- improve reliability with monitoring, diagnostics, and adaptive control

## Produkt und Service-Modell
Phase 1:
- robotics systems consulting
- prototype design and integration support
- technical architecture for perception, controls, and deployment

Phase 2:
- reusable robotics middleware and orchestration components
- monitoring dashboards and remote support tooling
- subscription-based software and maintenance packages

## Marktanalyse
Berlin offers strong access to deep-tech talent, startup networks, research institutions, and public innovation support. Across Germany, industrial digitization, warehouse modernization, and labor constraints create demand for robotics solutions that are easier to adopt and less capital-intensive than fully custom automation projects.

Primary target customers:
- SMEs in manufacturing
- warehouse and intralogistics operators
- robotics pilots inside industrial innovation teams
- companies needing applied AI plus physical automation integration

## Wettbewerbsanalyse
The market includes traditional automation integrators, robotics consultancies, and larger enterprise solution vendors. CyberBerlin Robotics GmbH differentiates itself through:
- focus on practical deployment rather than research-only robotics
- modular architecture that lowers integration friction
- combination of robotics, cloud infrastructure, and software product thinking
- Berlin-based innovation profile aligned with public funding and startup growth channels

## Vertriebskanäle
- founder-led business development
- pilot projects and technical advisory engagements
- partnership outreach to Berlin innovation, manufacturing, and logistics networks
- grant-supported early product development and visibility
- network-driven introductions through startup, deep-tech, and industrial communities

## Skalierbarkeit
The company is designed to move from consulting-supported technical validation into productized software assets and repeatable service packages. Scalability comes from:
- reusable robotics middleware and orchestration components
- standardized deployment patterns
- repeatable monitoring and remote support services
- a roadmap from custom integration into recurring software and maintenance revenue

## Team

## Financial Logic
Current operating model includes lean fixed costs plus targeted expert consulting support to accelerate technical validation. Additional founder equity and grant financing are expected to extend runway while core product and customer development milestones are reached.

## Near-Term Milestones

## Why Now
The convergence of robotics software maturity, AI-enabled perception, and industrial digitization creates a strong timing window for a focused Berlin robotics company. CyberBerlin Robotics GmbH aims to use this window to build a capital-efficient robotics business with strong innovation credibility and practical commercial application.
